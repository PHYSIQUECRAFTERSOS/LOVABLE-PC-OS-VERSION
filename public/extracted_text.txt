Tempo
The First number that appears in the "Tempo" is the Eccentric portion of the lift also known as the negative or the “lowering". If this number is a 2
(2-0-1-0) that means you will lower the weight for a count of 2 seconds.

The second number that appears in the "Tempo" is at the bottom of the lift and determines whether you will pause or not before changing
directions, this pause takes place when the muscle is fully stretched or in other words lengthened. For example if the tempo is (2-1-1-0) this
would mean that after you have lowered the weight for a count of 2, you will pause at the bottom for 1 second before changing directions,
contracting the desired muscle, and completing another rep.

The third number that appears in the "Tempo" is the Concentric portion of the lift, or the actual lifting of the weight itself. If this number is a 1 (2-
0-1-0) then you will lift the weight in a controlled manner taking 1 second to complete the rep from bottom to top. We don’t need to count 1 here
as usually just completing a rep at a standard pace (not explosively moving it as fast as possible) will result in roughly a 1 second count.

The final number that appears in the "Tempo" is the pause at peak contraction meaning the top of the lift or when the muscle is fully shortened. If
this number is a 1 (2-0-1-1) then you will pause for 1 second at the top of the rep, squeezing the muscle for a complete second before beginning
your eccentric which in this case would be 2s.
